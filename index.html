What is Hypnosis?

Hypnosis, which we often see in movies or stage performances, isn't about losing control or being tricked. Instead, it's a way to help people relax deeply and be open to suggestions. When someone is in hypnosis, they're in a special kind of relaxation where their subconscious mind is more open to positive ideas and thoughts.

You might wonder how hypnosis works. Well, during a hypnosis session, a trained therapist guides you into this relaxed state. It's like when you're daydreaming or really focused on something and everything elses fades away. In this state, your subconscious mind is more willing to listen to the therapist's suggestions. It's kind of like planting seeds in a garden - the therapist helps you plant positive thoughts in your mind.

Some people worry that hypnosis might make them do things they don't want to do, like in the movies. But that's not just true. You're always in control during hypnosis, and you can't be forced to do anything against your will. It's all about helping you relax and feel better, not about making you do something silly or embarrassing. So, if you're feeling stressed or overwhelmed, hypnosis might be just the thing to help you unwind and feel more at ease.
